Ending Corneal Blindness in India,
One transplant at a time.

5.6 million people suffer from corneal blindness in India

India is home to the greatest corneal blind population in the world. There are 1 million people with corneal blindness in both eyes and 4.6 million people with corneal blindness in one eye. Fortunately, India has developed enough of a public health infrastructure in order to address the problem including eye banks, trained surgeons, and hospital partnerships. The implementation of policies including mandatory notification and first person consent will help eradicate corneal blindness in the most deeply affected country in the world.
